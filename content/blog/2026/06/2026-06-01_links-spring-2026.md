[A walkthrough for the video game *Cave Story* in the form of a rap album](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?app=desktop&list=PLtEPz7pWO1AXLRlsUjbjXhyd9I-24zaJo). I mean this with zero irony whatsoever: there are some legit bangers here. The most impressive part to me is how it refuses to compromise on being both musically good and an actually usable guide to the game. 



[Austenland: The World's Most Immersive Austen Experience?](https://mssv.net/2026/04/01/austenland-the-worlds-most-immersive-austen-experience/). An exploration of a very strange (and now defunct) LARP experience in which (exclusively?) women were immersed in a Regency-era Jane Austen-esque high society for three weeks (!). After reading the first paragraph outlining the premise I thought "Wait, isn't a three-week roleplay experience where an attractive actor who never breaks kayfabe promises they'll love you forever going to be emotionally fraught?" As it turns out, yes!

Did you know that Facebook officially gave up on the VR component of its "metaverse" - you know, the *sine non qua* that makes it a metaverse as opposed to a shitty MMO - in January of this year?  [A post-mortem from Neal Stephenson](https://nealstephenson.substack.com/p/my-prodigal-brainchild), the sci-fi author who coined the word "Metaverse" (and had basically nothing to do with Zuck's stab at the concept) reflecting on the perils of using a narritive device from a 1992 novel that also features pizza delivery ninjas and an ancient Sumerian computer virus that can brick the human nervous system as a blueprint for actual technology.

[Kimi, author of the Menard](https://stash.buds.zone/view/1049):

And finally, the single strangest document I've seen published by a government-adjacent organization:  [an official publication of the UN Food and Agriculture Organization on how to farm snails](https://openknowledge.fao.org/server/api/core/bitstreams/e7a4e667-8da3-41f0-8c77-8a73dec277ca/content).

Every decision on presentation makes sense if you think about it. It's a document that will be translated into many languages; breaking it into short numbered snippets makes that process easier to do and to verify. The target audience is people in developing countries with low literacy rates, who maybe have only just recently become literate themselves, so using simple language peppered with diagrams is a given. And yet, in aggregate that series of reasonable choices alongside the subject matter has produced what I can only describe as a constant stream of surreal shitposts.
